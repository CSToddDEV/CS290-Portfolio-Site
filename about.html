Education

My collegiate education began after I graduated highschool in 2006. With the desire to save money I decided it was best to attend Commyunity College for two years so I could work.

In 2008 I was accepted to Virginia Tech. I pursued my Bachelor of Arts in English during my time there. I graduated with afocus in Creative Writing. At the time I had dreams of being the next Kerouac.

As life would have it, my dream to write the next American Novel has not manifested yet. However, I did get to spend a lot of time "On the Road", traveling the United States.

Tech has always been a huge part of my life, and I have been coding since I was young. So when I made the decision to switch careers, a tech focused career was the natural choice!

With that I came to Oregon State University, where I am pursuing my Bachelors of Science in Comptuer Science. I hope to graduate December 2021!

I do plan to pursue a Masters degree in the field right after graduation. Right now I am looking heavily at The Georgia Tech program, with a focus in Mahcine Learning.

Hospitality

Ah, the restaurant and hospitality industry. If you have ever worked in it, you know it's a monster. For the uninitiated, it is one of the most trying and intense jobs one can have at a high level

In my 14 years (I started at 18) I have been a server, bartender, bar manager, bar director, award-winning sommelier, and general manager for a 4.5 million dollar restaurant. It has been a ride. But I have learned a lot!

More than anything, the industry has taught me amazing soft skills. When you have to communicate with people of all kinds, from all different walks of life, and of (lets be fair here) varying levels of intelligence... all while there hungry, you develop a style of communication that can't be taught. It is genuine and earnest, and by far the thing I am most grateful for from the industry.

Not to mention it has taught me to pick an aweosme bottle of wine of a wine list, and my cocktails will leave you wanting one to many.
